Design Principle

Designed for families who distrust screens

Screen as enabler, not destination

Sessions have a hard 2-hour limit enforced server-side. There is no "one more subject" hook. When Emma's session ends, Bede says goodbye and the screen goes quiet — the same way a book closes. The sidebar's session bar is the only timer a student sees, and only when approaching the limit.

No dopamine engineering

No streaks. No badges. No sound effects for correct answers. No celebration animations. The celebrate_discovery tool card appears quietly — a thin left-border accent on a parchment background. The reward is understanding, not the interface. A child who answered well will know it from Bede's words, not a fireworks display.

The books are the point

Bede exists to extend the living books shelf, not replace it. Every subject opener references the actual books, poems, and nature observations that happened offline. The sidebar's unit card and faith emphasis are reminders that this conversation is downstream of a morning that started with a walk and a chapter read aloud.
